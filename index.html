About

At fourteen, I became the youngest Indian to compete and medal at the International Olympiad in Artificial Intelligence, earning Bronze in Beijing. I earned my spot after a four-stage national selection process and a 10-day training camp at IIIT Hyderabad — with lectures from Microsoft, Adobe, Amazon, and IBM researchers.

I contribute research at Emergence AI, focusing on agentic AI evaluation, trust frameworks, and MCP benchmarking. I'm also interested in code representation learning and computer vision. Before all of this, I was twelve and self-taught — scoring a 1510 SAT, clearing AP Computer Science with a perfect 5, and passing IGCSE Mathematics Extended with an A.

Outside research, I'm a TEDx speaker, Model UN delegate, music producer, and sustainability advocate. I believe the most interesting problems sit at the intersection of disciplines.

Research & Projects

01
Decoupling Reasoning from Action: Architectural Impacts on Agentic Planning Consistency
Evaluates three LLM-agent context architectures — monolithic, partially partitioned, and fully role-separated (Planner, Executor, Memory) — using MCPBench with GPT-4o across six ablation studies. Finds that role-separated architectures offer superior stability in logical dependency tracking under high-interference conditions.
